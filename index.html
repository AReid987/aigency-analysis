What the System Actually Is

01
Blackout: AI-Powered Trading Infrastructure
A 14-agent SLM scanning pipeline with weighted scoring, genetic algorithm strategy updates, rig block checks, and HITL approval — not an alpha-seeking fund
MEV is an infrastructure game ($5K-$20K/month bare-metal), not an intelligence game. 73% of automated trading accounts fail within 6 months. Axiom ($500K seed → $390M revenue) proves the viable model is trading terminals, not alpha-seeking funds. The system includes a rig block mechanism that pauses trading after three consecutive losses and triggers a genetic algorithm review process. A chrome extension feeds signals from a Padre terminal, capturing accept/reject decisions and outcomes for continuous learning. Participants are heavily vetted and receive no ownership — only a limited-time percentage of portfolio profits based on their deposit. The trading operation is closed to the public and functions as proof-of-concept for the intelligence infrastructure.
02
Forge: Self-Directed Learning Environment
CBT-informed suggestions and feedback in a collaborative environment — not therapy, not licensed clinical practice
Emotional AI in education has 84.4% student acceptance but suicides linked to Character.AI and wrongful death lawsuits are forming. The system provides CBT methods that anyone can use themselves, delivered through subtle suggestions, feedback, and ideas in collaboration with the learner — not as therapeutic advice or licensed clinical service. The Pedagogy of the Oppressed + Bloom's + Constructivism + Agile cocktail remains genuinely novel with zero implemented precedents. EdTech PMF requires 8-18 months, so the 7-14 day sprint model must be adapted: use sprints for content/modules, not for full product validation.
03
Agent Swarm: JIT Tool-Shed Architecture
Agents are spawned just-in-time for specific tasks, run in parallel where possible, and retire with memory captured; most agents idle or on heartbeat cron schedules
The architecture operates as a tool shed: agents are spawned just-in-time for specific tasks, execute in parallel where possible, and retire with memory captured before deactivation. Most agents remain idle or operate on heartbeat cron schedules — ZENITH at 5-10 minute intervals, the LIBRARIAN at 1x per hour. This is fundamentally different from a continuously running concurrent swarm. DeepMind scaling laws (17.2x error amplification in flat networks) still apply to active coordination sets, but the hierarchical topology + cron-based heartbeat schedules (ZENITH 5-10 min, LIBRARIAN 1x/hour) with most agents asleep changes the risk profile from CRITICAL to MANAGEABLE. The genetic algorithm retirement + memory capture system is elegant organizational design.
04
AGFA: Inter-Node Communication Token
Functions as gas for child-to-parent communications in the graft network; Solana-based, not ETH; Crystal Graft concept is early-stage and evolving
AGFA is designed as gas for child-to-parent communications within the graft network, not as a speculative investment vehicle. The Crystal Graft concept — an on-chain validated knowledge package — is in early-stage design and expected to evolve significantly before any harvest becomes viable. Solana is the likely chain for all on-chain components, not Ethereum or Base. The token design is tied to a clear operational utility (inter-node communication fees), which changes the regulatory and economic risk profile compared to speculative tokens.
05
Governance: Human-in-the-Loop Architecture
Kill switches armed, full observability, multi-timeframe reporting, HITL approval gates — documented escalation protocols for regulatory compliance
The oversight architecture includes: daily/weekly/monthly/quarterly reports delivered to the founder, workspaces streaming outputs to all devices including remote access, kill switches armed and accessible at all times, full observability on all devices even when the founder is AFK, HITL approval required before trades execute, rig block checks on a long list of parameters, quality gates and verification steps required for every task, and chrome extension feedback loops that capture trade accept/reject decisions and their outcomes. This is a genuine human-in-the-loop system, not a temporary workaround. The escalation thresholds ($100 → $500 → $2K → Architect) are the oversight architecture that regulators require. The framework includes documented protocols with timestamps, audit trails, and the legal wrapper (Marshall Islands Digital LLC recommended) to formalize the structure. The founder maintains kill switch authority, veto power over constitutional changes, and dissolution authority at all times — this is a legal necessity, not a transitional role.

Key Architecture Insights

Substrate diversity is the core competitive moat. Unique substrates (OpenClaw, gptme, Motia, Letta, ZeroClaw) per agent reduce correlated errors. This is hard to replicate and should be documented as IP.

Agent drift after 73 interactions. For sales at 15 leads/day, that's ~5 days. The cron-based rotation + memory capture system is the correct approach — treating agent drift as an organizational design problem rather than a software bug.

Hierarchical topology mandatory. Flat networks amplify errors 17.2x. The ZENITH→VECTOR→RING→SQUAD→CLUSTER→MESH vector architecture with gossip protocols is the right design.
